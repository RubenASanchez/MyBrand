About Me

The ability for people to communicate with each other is something that I value greatly. I remember a moment in grade school when I found some children playing. I wanted to join in but I could not understand their words. They couldn't understand mine. As children often did, and do, we innovated. We pointed, gestured, and laughed. We had fun. It is this freedom and desire for open and successful communication that I hope to maintain in all of my endeavors.

My research interests involve integrating phonetic perception methodologies with brain imaging technologies such as fMRI and MEG in order to investigate neuronal circuitry in multilinguals.

Hi, my name is Ruben A. Sanchez. I recently graduated from the University of California, Santa Cruz. I am a researcher focusing in Linguistics and Neuroscience. In particular I am interested in the neuronal circuits involved in phonetic perception and production. With the use of brain imaging technologies such as EEG, fMRI, and MEG the dynamic nature of these networks can be captured. Knowledge about the collaborative relationships between and amongst various brain regions can be used to improve language teaching methods, better program internet search engines, as well as help find treatments for speech language pathologies. Feel free to contact me to learn more.
